# Numerical experiments with a poro-viscoelastic continuum model for gels

## 摘要

<p>The mechanical behavior of many hydrogels is governed by the coupled effects of viscoelasticity, arising from molecular rearrangements, and poroelasticity, due to fluid diffusion through the network. In this work, we develop a fully three-dimensional poro-viscoelastic model formulated with the equilibrium swollen state as the reference configuration. The model is consistent with thermodynamic constraints and capable of capturing complex time-dependent responses observed in polymeric, fibrous and brush gels. Through numerical simulations, we demonstrate that the model reproduces experimentally observed phenomena, including filament thinning in extensional rheology of polymeric gels and stress relaxation in step-wise compression of fibrous gels. Furthermore, we show that for brush gels with fixed small-strain shear modulus, mechanical behavior is governed by two key parameters: G 2, which modulates rate-dependent effects, and β, which controls the degree of strain stiffening. The model exhibits limitations in accurately capturing strong tension-induced stiffening and tensile dilation observed in certain brush gels due to the assumption of isochoricity in viscoelasticity. Finally, we show that hysteresis behavior under cyclic compression and indentation exhibits a non-monotonic dependence on the loading rate, governed by the intrinsic relaxation timescale of the network.</p>
