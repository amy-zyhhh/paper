## 摘要

<p>Wrinkling behaviors are widely observed from micro- to macro-scale materials and structures. Finite element method (FEM)-based numerical methods for simulating the detailed 3D morphology of wrinkles include implicit nonlinear buckling analysis and explicit pseudo-dynamics. However, the convergence is hard to achieve, and computation is inefficient for a large number of DOFs. This paper proposes an innovative and efficient approach to obtain 3D wrinkling morphologies of isotropic membranes using 2D FEM results obtained from Tension Field Theory (TFT), and leveraging a conditional Generative Adversarial Network (cGAN) to achieve real-time prediction. Three main contributions of this study include: (i) an efficient 3D wrinkle database for training is constructed according to the wrinkling strain concept and a physically consistent constraint considering Poisson correction; (ii) the cGAN is generalized by designing a deep-convolutional conditional architecture and setting a hybrid loss function balancing adversarial loss and pixel-level L1 reconstruction; (iii) a fine-tuning technology able to integrate scarce experimental data or theoretical result is employed to improve the computational accuracy. The established cGAN is validated by two typical examples, which show that the computational efficiency is improved by several orders of magnitude, compared with the FEM-based post-buckling analysis. The study presents a promising AI-enhanced paradigm for the simulation of wrinkling, which encourages us to develop a powerful wrinkle generator in the future by collecting millions of training data, including a variety of geometries, loads and boundary conditions. The proposed method is also expected to be extended to orthotropic or hyperelastic thin films in finite strain.</p>
