# Trapped fluids and dry zones: Adhesion’s role in soft lubrication instabilities

## 摘要

<p>Recent experiments have shown that adhesive interactions play a significant role in soft lubrication, introducing instabilities that drastically alter contact dynamics. These interactions disrupt fluid flow and generate surface undulations on soft substrates subjected to normal contact, creating dry zones and pockets which trap fluid and can significantly increase drainage time. In sliding experiments, such instabilities are found to trigger a transition from the elasto-hydrodynamic lubrication (EHL) regime to the mixed lubrication regime—even on smooth surfaces where roughness is negligible compared to the fluid gap thickness. In this study, we incorporate adhesive forces into the Reynolds lubrication theory to study instabilities arising during normal contact between a rigid surface and a soft substrate. A linear stability analysis reveals a dimensionless parameter, α, that predicts when small surface perturbations can grow and becomes unstable. To investigate the nonlinear evolution of these instabilities, we develop a numerical solver that couples a finite difference method for the Reynolds equation with a finite element method for elastic deformation of the substrate. Our simulations show that when α &gt; 1, adhesion alone can induce &quot;dry&quot; contact zones—regions with nearly zero fluid thickness—separated by pockets of trapped fluid.</p>
