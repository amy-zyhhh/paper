# A study of short-crack growth behaviour in an AM Ti alloy using an anisotropic phase field model coupled with crystal plasticity

## 摘要

<p>This work investigates the fundamental failure mechanism associated with the growth of micro-structurally small cracks in an α′-phase Ti alloy fabricated by additive manufacturing (AM). Such alloy is used in critical components of aero-engines, where the uncertainty in the assessment about how long it will take for micro-structurally small cracks to grow to a critical size is crucial to the prediction and improvement of their operating lifetime. To that purpose, a new anisotropic phase field model coupled with non-local crystal plasticity is developed to study the effects of microstructural features of the additive manufactured α′-Ti alloy on the growth behaviour of small cracks. The coupled thermodynamic based formulation has been implemented numerically into the finite element method and used to study the behaviour of micro-structurally small cracks growing from AM defects. The results of the modelling work enabled the identification of (i) the best metric to define the local crack growth direction (i.e. maximal principal stress direction and normal to the plane of maximum accumulated plastic slip), (ii) the role played by the local stored strain energy as a driving force for small crack nucleation and growth from AM defects in the Ti alloy, and (iii) the effect of the AM microstructural features in general on the formation of hot spots or microcrack initiation sites.</p>
