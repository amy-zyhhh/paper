# Enhanced Poynting effect in torsion of a polydomain liquid crystal elastomer cylinder

## 摘要

<p>In nonlinear elasticity, the Poynting effect refers to the generation of a normal (axial) stress or deformation under shear or torsional loading. Recent finite element simulations have reported a markedly enhanced Poynting effect in the torsion of isotropic polydomain liquid crystal elastomer (LCE) cylinders compared with neo-Hookean solids. In this work, we present an analytical and experimental study of the Poynting effect in LCEs subjected to torsion. Our analyses show that, under simple shear, a polydomain LCE exhibits the same ratio of normal stress to applied shear stress as that of a neo-Hookean material. In contrast, for an elastomeric cylinder under torsion, both analytical predictions and experimental measurements demonstrate a significantly larger ratio of axial force to applied torque in LCEs. We further show that, for both LCE and neo-Hookean cylinders under torsion, although individual stress components are rate- or time-dependent, the ratio between the normal force and the applied moment is insensitive to the loading rate or time. These results elucidate the distinct nonlinear coupling between shear and normal responses in LCEs and clarify the origin of their enhanced Poynting effect under torsional deformation.</p>
