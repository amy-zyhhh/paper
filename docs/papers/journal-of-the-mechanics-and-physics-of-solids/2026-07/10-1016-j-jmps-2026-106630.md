# Dynamics and modal energy analysis of periodic mechanism-based structures with elastic hinges

## 摘要

<p>Mechanisms featuring ideal hinges, which allow free rotation, exhibit unique mathematical and physical properties. In this work, we replace the ideal hinges with elastic ones that can transmit torques, thereby enhancing the mechanical coupling within the system. We analyze the dynamics of the proposed mechanism-based structures with elastic hinges through numerical simulations, theoretical modeling, and experimental measurements. Our results demonstrate that the strategic placement of elastic hinges significantly alters the dispersion relations and governs the behavior of zero-group-velocity (ZGV) modes. Transient simulations, along with spectral and wavefield analysis of the finite structures, reveal strong wave localization at the ZGV modes, highlighting their potential for application in energy harvesting. Moreover, based on a newly defined modal energy fraction of the torsional springs, we analyze low-order modal behaviors and examine two limiting cases within the theoretical framework: one combining ideal hinges with torsional springs, and the other in the absence of torsional springs. The results demonstrate that torsional springs (associated with bending deformations of elastic hinges) dominate the low-order modes and the resulting modal energy fraction of the torsional springs can be effectively controlled by altering the structural parameters. The proposed modal energy analysis presented herein opens new avenues for the development of elastic metamaterials with elastic hinges.</p>
