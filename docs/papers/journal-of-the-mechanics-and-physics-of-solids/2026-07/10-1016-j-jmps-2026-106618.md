## 摘要

<p>Topological mechanics enables robust, scattering-immune wave transport, yet its extension from flat lattices to intrinsically curved structures remains limited. Here, we show that curvature acts as an intrinsic geometric parameter for controlling topological phases in elastic metamaterials. Starting from shell mechanics, we derive an effective four-band, mirror-protected Su-Schrieffer-Heeger (SSH)-type model revealing how curvature and thickness renormalize the effective couplings. Using a topological optimization framework, we design continuum shell unit cells that undergo curvature and thickness driven transitions among a trivial insulator, a Dirac-like semimetal (SMs), and a topological insulator phases (TIs). Full-wave simulations confirm localized edge modes in the non-trivial regime, their robustness against defects, twisting, and mechanical deformation, and the curvature-tuned creation, migration, and annihilation of Dirac points. These results establish curvature as an intrinsic design degree of freedom, enabling mechanically programmable topological waveguides on elastic shells for reconfigurable phononic and wearable devices.</p>
