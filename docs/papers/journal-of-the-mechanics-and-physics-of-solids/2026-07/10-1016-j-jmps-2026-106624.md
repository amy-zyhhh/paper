# Unraveling microstructure evolution and constitutive behavior of AISI 9310 steel in ultrasonic vibration-assisted tension via combined molecular dynamics and experimental insights

## 摘要

<p>Ultrasonic vibration (UV) induced acoustic softening effect is widely utilized in manufacturing to enhance process performance and reduce energy consumption. However, the underlying microstructure evolution mechanism of acoustic plasticity remains controversial due to challenges in directly observing the dynamic behavior with high-frequency vibration. Here ultrasonic vibration-assisted tensile (UVAT) molecular dynamics simulations and the nudged elastic band method are employed to investigate the microscopic mechanism of intermittent dislocation motion and dislocation-grain boundary interactions. Concurrently integrating the UVAT experiment of AISI 9310 steel with crystal plasticity theory, we systematically analyze the crystal plastic deformation assisted by dislocation emission and the influence of unique microstructural evolution on mechanical responses. The dislocation glide barrier decreases under UV by energy landscape calculations. Ultrasonic-induced stress oscillations and atomic potential fluctuations enhance dislocation mobility, thereby promoting dislocation-grain boundary interactions to suppress dislocation pile-up and reduce dislocation density. Dislocation rapid diffusion facilitates lattice rotation, reducing low-angle grain boundary fraction and weakening deformation texture through microscopic characterization by electron backscatter diffraction (EBSD). UV reduces flow stress and strain hardening through dislocation density suppression, activation energy lowering, and stress superposition.</p>
