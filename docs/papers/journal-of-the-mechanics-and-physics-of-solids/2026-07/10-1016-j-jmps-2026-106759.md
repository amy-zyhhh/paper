# Strain gradient plasticity-driven hydrogen transport and embrittlement in polycrystalline Ti-6Al-4V: A study coupling crystal plasticity and phase-field method

## 摘要

<p>Hydrogen embrittlement severely degrades the ductility and fracture resistance of metallic components in energy and transportation sectors. This degradation stems from the complex interplay between hydrogen transport, dislocation-mediated plasticity, and fracture. Prevailing continuum models predominantly treat hydrogen transport as stress-assisted diffusion coupled with local dislocation trapping, thereby neglecting the convective transport of hydrogen by moving dislocations within a strain gradient plasticity (SGP) framework. This omission can lead to a significant underestimation of hydrogen accumulation and stress intensification at critical microstructural features like grain boundaries and crack tips. To bridge this gap, a thermodynamically consistent chemo-mechanical-damage model is proposed. This framework unifies a nonlocal dislocation-density-based crystal plasticity (CP) model, a hydrogen diffusion and trapping model, and a phase-field fracture model. In the present model, the effect of dislocation flux is incorporated and explicitly coupled with the hydrogen flux associated with trap sites, enabling the simultaneous description of lattice diffusion, dislocation-mediated trapping, and SGP-driven hydrogen transport. This extends the framework toward capturing hydrogen-assisted decohesion in polycrystalline Ti-6Al-4V under uniaxial tension. The results systematically quantify the respective roles of geometrically necessary dislocations (GNDs) and statistically stored dislocations (SSDs) in hydrogen trapping and demonstrate that SGP-driven hydrogen transport substantially enhances hydrogen enrichment at grain boundaries and crack tips, thereby accelerating crack initiation, and propagation, intensifying the propensity for intergranular fracture. This work provides a unified grain-scale framework for predicting hydrogen embrittlement and highlights the critical, yet previously underestimated, role of SGP in hydrogen-assisted fracture processes.</p>
