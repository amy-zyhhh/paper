# Frequency-domain Bayesian inference for identifying high-dimensional nonlinear mechanical properties of heterogeneous materials

## 摘要

<p>Inferring material properties and mechanical parameters from full-field measurements has been a central challenge in mechanics, with wide applications ranging from elastography to aerospace damage detection. The inevitable measurement noise exacerbates the ill-posedness of these inverse problems, necessitating robust uncertainty quantification method. Bayesian inference offers a principled methodology to recast these inverse problems within a statistical framework. However, it faces significant difficulties when the inferred spatial distributions of mechanical properties are represented in high-dimensional discrete forms (“curse of dimensionality”) and/or when highly nonlinear systems with limited sensitivity information for posterior approximations are involved. To address these two challenges, we develop a novel method, named frequency-domain Bayesian inference. This method projects a high-dimensional Bayesian problem into a low-dimensional representation in the frequency domain, and leverages differentiable programming techniques to enable automatic sensitivity analysis during posterior sampling of mechanical parameters. The proposed method operates without any prior datasets and achieves accurate inference with reliable uncertainty estimates, even when only a single experimental measurement is available. Its generality and accuracy are validated across four categories of inverse mechanical problems, including elastic modulus identification, plastic yield stress field identification, external load reconstruction, and thermal conductivity estimation. It is worth noting that the identification of plastic yield stress fields demonstrates the method’s capability to handle history-dependent circumstance with strong nonlinearities. Additional experiments with digital image correlation measurements are further conducted to highlight the practical applications of the proposed method.</p>

<p><strong>为什么做：</strong>从全场测量反演材料参数是力学中的核心挑战，但测量噪声加剧了反问题的不适定性，需要鲁棒的不确定性量化方法。传统贝叶斯推理在处理高维离散化参数场（维度诅咒）和/或强非线性系统（难以获得后验近似所需的梯度信息）时面临巨大困难。现有基于生成模型（如GAN）的降维方法依赖大量先验数据训练，且复杂非线性问题的梯度分析（如伴随法）推导繁琐。本文旨在克服这些局限性。</p>
<p><strong>怎么做：</strong>方法结合了离散余弦变换（DCT）和可微分编程技术。1. 利用DCT的能量压缩特性，将空间域的高维参数场投影到频率域的低维系数表示，实现无训练数据的降维。2. 采用可微分编程技术，在基于Hamiltonian Monte Carlo（HMC）的后验采样过程中，自动计算似然函数对频率域系数的梯度，避免了手动推导灵敏度。3. 工作流程包括：通过优化确定归一化参数，在频率域进行HMC采样获得后验分布，最后将样本转换回空间域进行统计分析。方法在弹性、塑性（历史相关非线性）、外载荷识别和热传导四类反问题上进行了验证，使用了数值仿真（如FEM）和实验数据（如DIC测量）。</p>
<p><strong>做得怎么样：</strong>方法在多种测试案例中表现出色：1. 能够在仅有一次实验测量、无先验数据集的情况下，实现高精度的参数场识别和可靠的不确定性估计。2. 成功处理了强非线性的历史相关塑性屈服应力场识别问题。3. 与空间域贝叶斯推理方法相比，在计算效率（墙钟时间）和精度（L1误差）上均有显著优势，尤其在高分辨率（如128x128）下优势更明显。4. 通过DIC实验验证了其实际应用能力，能清晰识别软夹杂的形状。原文通过系统对比（不同噪声水平、保留系数数量、分辨率）和误差分析充分评价了方法性能。</p>
