# Theory-guided design of continuous-gradient architected materials: Trapping impact energy via impedance valley

## 摘要

<p>Impedance gradient provides an effective lever to reduce transmitted pressure under impulse loading. However, discrete multilayer designs are prone to interfacial delamination, whereas continuous hard‑to‑soft gradient designs still promote rear‑face energy concentration. Here we introduce a continuous-gradient architected material featuring a valley‑shaped impedance profile that activates an impact energy trap and suppresses rear‑face localization without creating material interfaces. The impedance gradient design is realized using a density gradient Gyroid structure. An innovative theoretical model which considers the nonmonotonic spatial distribution of mechanical properties is developed to elucidate the propagation of nonlinear stress waves in continuous-gradient materials and efficiently predict the transmitted pressure. The results show that the pressure attenuation of impedance‑valley designs outperforms the conventional design with a linear impedance distribution. Further analysis reveals that the designed impedance valley and material strain softening behavior enable energy retention within the structures by forming an impact energy trap. Subsequently, parametric studies indicate that the optimal depth and location of impedance valleys exist to improve attenuation. Finally, shock-tube experiments validate the enhanced impulse-mitigation performance of impedance-valley design, demonstrating a 33% reduction in protected-side peak pressure compared with conventional linear impedance gradients.</p>
