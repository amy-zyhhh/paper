# Hamiltonian simulation of elastodynamics on a quantum computer via energy conservation mapping

## 摘要

<p>Simulating elastic wave propagation is a fundamental task in solid mechanics. By using a quantum computer, Hamiltonian simulation provides a physically different approach for this task. This approach requires mapping the governing equations of elastodynamics to a Schrödinger equation, which can then be solved efficiently on a quantum computer. To achieve this goal, we introduce an energy conservation mapping that applies to conservative elastodynamics in one, two, and three dimensions. The core principle is to define a quantum state vector whose squared norm is directly proportional to the total mechanical energy of the elastodynamics. The physical law of energy conservation then naturally guarantees the mapping to a Schrödinger equation. Numerical examples on a quantum simulator are performed to verify the effectiveness of the mapping. Furthermore, the method is also demonstrated on a real superconducting quantum processor, obtaining results with moderate accuracy under the effects of quantum noise. We believe this work presents a meaningful step toward applying quantum computing to solid mechanics.</p>
