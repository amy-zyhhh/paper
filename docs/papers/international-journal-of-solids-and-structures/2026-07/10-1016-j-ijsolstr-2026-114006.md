# Tailoring tensile responses of trilayer heterostructures

## 摘要

<p>We perform uniaxial tensile tests on sandwich-like trilayer heterostructures, in which the middle layers are relatively brittle compared to the lateral layers. A variety of tensile responses are observed before the fracture of the lateral layers, including both monotonic and non-monotonic force–displacement curves. The non-monotonic one typically features a plateau region, which may be flat or fluctuating. By integrating experiments, analytical modeling, and finite element analysis, we demonstrate that the variety of tensile responses arises from the competition between interface and bulk fracture. Then, the tensile response can be tailored by rationally designing the interfacial and material properties based on our analytical solution. Moreover, our analysis reveals a significant size effect on the tensile response. Leveraging this effect, we propose a fracture engineering strategy to proactively modulate the response. For example, the increase in the number of uniformly distributed pre-cracks, which are intentionally introduced within the middle layer, can decrease the plateau length to zero and reduce the tendency of the middle layer to fracture. This process is customizable, theoretically predictable, and experimentally feasible. Furthermore, the tunable tensile response implies that the energy dissipation capability can be tailored accordingly. We anticipate that our strategies can be broadly generalized to guide the rational design of diverse heterostructures.</p>

<h3>主要结论</h3>
<ul><li>三层异质结构的拉伸响应多样性源于界面断裂与中间层体断裂之间的竞争。</li>
<li>基于解析解，可以通过理性设计界面性能和材料属性来定制拉伸力-位移曲线。</li>
<li>拉伸响应存在显著的尺寸效应，利用此效应（如引入均匀分布的预裂纹）可主动调控响应，例如消除平台区或降低中间层断裂倾向。</li>
<li>可调的拉伸响应意味着能量耗散能力也可相应地被定制。</li></ul>
