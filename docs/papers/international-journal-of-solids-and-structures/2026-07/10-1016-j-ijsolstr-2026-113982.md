# Surface inertia effects on effective dynamic properties of SH-wave in unidirectional porous materials

## 摘要

<p>In anti-plane dynamic boundary value problems that incorporate surface effects, the boundary conditions encompass not only surface elasticity but also surface inertia. The surface inertia is directly quantified by the parameter of surface mass density. However, due to the challenges in measuring the surface mass density and the increased complexity of boundary conditions when this parameter is introduced, most existing literature on the dynamic boundary value problems with surface effects tends to omit the surface inertia-either without explanation or with only brief justification. While this simplification facilitates problem-solving, it limits deeper investigation into the phenomenon. Moreover, recent studies have demonstrated that the surface inertia can significantly influence the dynamic mechanical behavior of structures at micro-and nano-scales. This paper investigates the effective dynamic properties of SH-wave by cylindrical porous structures with surface effects using the generalized self-consistent method. We find that the surface inertia significantly reduces both the effective SH-wave phase velocity and the effective axial shear modulus, while simultaneously enhancing the effective attenuation. This demonstrates that the surface inertia plays a non-negligible role in influencing the dynamic properties of porous structures.</p>
