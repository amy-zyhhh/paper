## 摘要

<p>An energy-based symplectic Hamiltonian framework is developed to derive an exact analytical solution for stress and deformation responses of anisotropic auxetic beams. Architected auxetic materials exhibit extreme extension–shear and bending–shear couplings that fundamentally violate the kinematic assumptions underlying classical and higher-order beam theories, while purely numerical approaches obscure the governing physical mechanisms. In this study, the two-dimensional elasticity framework of a homogenized anti-tetrachiral lattice is reformulated as a canonical Hamiltonian system and solved via symplectic eigendecomposition without ad hoc kinematic assumptions or shear correction factors. The proposed formulation provides exact closed-form representations of cross-sectional warping, coupled stress redistribution, and boundary-layer decay, offering physical insights unavailable to existing beam theories. Analytical predictions show excellent agreement with high-fidelity finite element simulations across a wide range of slenderness ratios. Parametric investigations reveal two key non-classical behaviors intrinsic to auxetic architected beams: (i) chiral microstructural topology induces asymmetric stress distributions through the thickness, and (ii) orientation-dependent stiffness exhibits a boundary-condition-dependent reversal driven by coupling-induced membrane stress. The framework establishes a unified, energy-consistent, and computationally efficient analytical tool for predicting and interpreting the mechanics of anisotropic auxetic beams. It provides a rigorous theoretical foundation for geometry- and orientation-programmed stiffness tailoring and stress management in next-generation architected and metamaterial structures.</p>
