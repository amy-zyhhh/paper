# An investigation on damage and fracture mechanisms of PBX under extrusion load using ordinary state-based peridynamics

## 摘要

<p>The dynamic damage and fracture of polymer-bonded explosives (PBXs) are closely related to their ignition behaviors and therefore it is of critical importance for understanding the safety of PBXs. Taking the PBX extruded from a hole as an example, this study focuses on the mechanical damage and fracture behaviors of PBX and associated mechanisms by means of a novel rate-dependent ordinary state-based peridynamic (OSB-PD) model. The accuracy and robustness of the model were validated through some classic numerical examples such as the Kalthoff-Winkler impact fracture test. Then, the complete processes of PBX damage and fracture under extrusion load was systematically simulated. Numerical results indicate that significant stress concentration occurs around the hole during extrusion, with fracture initiating in regions with relatively large equivalent plastic strain. Subsequently, a conical slip zone within the PBX is formed accompanying by the severely damaged materials flow into the hole. With increased extrusion velocity, the PBX in the central region undergoes the large macroscopic movement along the previously formed slip surfaces, ultimately developing into a double-conical slip mode. Moreover, with increased extrusion velocity and hole diameter, the slip surface tends to incline towards the loading direction, the angle between slip surface and horizontal direction, which is named as the “slip angle”, also increases. This research exhibits that the developed rate-dependent OSB-PD model can be an effective numerical technique for in-depth understanding of the failure behaviors of PBXs under complex loading conditions, holding theoretical and engineering significance for the safety design and assessment of charge structures.</p>
