# Porous bionic nested tubular structures of luffa sponge with orthogonal gradient auxetic layers: design and energy absorption characteristics

## 摘要

<p>Lightweight luffa sponge with nested tubular morphology shows promising in energy absorption enhancement. However, its structural extreme complexity hinders the development of manufacturable bio-inspired structures, requiring concise modelling. In this study, a novel nested bio-inspired tubular (NBT) structure, driven from the orthogonal and gradient fiber distributions on the inner and outer walls of luffa sponge and core support system, is designed and fabricated by additive manufacturing. The reentrant configurations are integrated into the inner and outer layers of NBT in axial and hoop directions for their tunable anisotropic mechanical properties. The energy absorption mechanism of NBT is systematically investigated by compressive experiments and validated finite element (FE) models. A comprehensive comparison is conducted between the present NBT and its constituent parts, as well as NBTs with different designs to provide optimal design principle. Further, the influences of key geometric parameters of the optimal NBT design are explored, including the number of connecting ribs, the porosity difference of the inner and outer layers, and the interesting gradient porosity. The results demonstrate that axial gradient design boosts energy absorption efficiency by 44% compared to the uniform structure. The findings provide a valuable foundation for designing and optimizing advanced energy-absorbing tubular structures for protective engineering applications</p>
