# An analytical-driven multiscale modeling method for the mode I fracture behavior of fiber-reinforced cementitious composites

## 摘要

<p>Although various cross-scale models have been developed for fiber-reinforced cementitious composites (FRCCs), achieving a balance between physical fidelity and computational efficiency remains challenging. To address this, this study establishes a multi-scale modelling method driven by microscopic interfacial mechanics analytical theories. It develops a multi‑stage progressive analytical model covering interfacial shear stress, fiber pull‑out, crack bridging, and component tension, while quantitatively characterizing cross‑scale mechanical behavior under mild to moderate corrosion conditions through the introduction of corrosion coefficients. On this basis, a theory‑driven numerical method is proposed: utilizing derived analytical solutions for the single fiber pull‑out to define the nonlinear connector elements in ABAQUS. With a self‑developed three‑dimensional stochastic fiber generation algorithm, automated construction of finite element models for FRCC components is achieved. This research quantitatively captures the evolutionary modulation laws of corrosion coefficients χ and η c on fiber pull‑out load and macroscopic tensile stress. The proposed numerical method accurately reproduces the complex influence of fiber inclination, correctly reflecting the non-monotonic evolution of peak loads caused by snubbing and matrix spalling. Furthermore, the failure modes of fibers are investigated, further demonstrating the regulatory effects of yield strength on such failure behavior. Experimental validation via a dog-bone specimen confirms the model’s robustness and efficiency in reproducing concurrent fiber debonding and fracture. This study introduces a generalizable computational modeling method for the high-fidelity analysis of complex FRCC systems.</p>
