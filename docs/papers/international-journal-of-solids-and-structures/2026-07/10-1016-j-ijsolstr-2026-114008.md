# Membrane wrinkling model for incremental constitutive models based on a plane stress projection procedure and a dynamic relaxation method

## 摘要

<p>The plastic films of stratospheric balloon envelopes, obtained by extrusion, have anisotropic behavior which affects the appearance of wrinkles during the inflation of these balloons. The aim of this paper is to present a new membrane model, named PS-DPS-V2, which allows to simulate wrinkling phenomena of films within the framework of an incremental resolution of nonlinear constitutive laws. The “Plane Stress-Double Plane Stress” PS-DPS-V2 model is the evolution of a previous wrinkling model called PS-DPS, intended only for the isotropic material behavior. A projection technique based on the Newton–Raphson (NR) method allows control of the in-plane stress state and in-plane contraction for this type of material behavior. Based on an approach using the Almansi deformation formulation, this model is also able to simulate membrane thickness changes. Numerical instabilities due to the occurrence of wrinkles are resolved using the Dynamic Relaxation (DR) method integrating kinetic damping procedures. Results of several benchmark problems available in the literature are compared with this new wrinkling model in using two constitutive incremental models: an orthotropic elastic behavior and a non-linear hyperelasto-hysteresis model. The inflation of a square cushion composed of an anisotropic plastic film are also carried out experimentally and the formation of wrinkles on the cushion and the deformations obtained are compared with the results of the PS-DPS-V2 model. Comparisons with literature and experimental results show that the PS-DPS-V2 model is capable of accurately determining the distribution of wrinkles and stress values for orthotropic and nonlinear behavior models.</p>
