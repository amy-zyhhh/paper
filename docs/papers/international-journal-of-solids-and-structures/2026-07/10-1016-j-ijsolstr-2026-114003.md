# A multiphysically-based micromechanical model for time-evolutionary piezoresistive behaviors of viscoelastic porous nanocomposites

## 摘要

<p>Carbon-based polymer nanocomposites have emerged as promising flexible sensing materials due to their intrinsic electrical conductivity, high sensitivity, and porous microstructures-enhanced electromechanical performance, enabling advanced applications in health monitoring and intelligent robotics. Experimentally, such piezoresistive sensors frequently exhibit pronounced resistance creep and relaxation subjected to step and cyclic loadings, thereby compromising sensing accuracy and stability. Owing to the heterogeneity of viscoelastic porous nanocomposites (VPNs), the time-dependent piezoresistivity cannot be attributed solely to the inherent viscoelasticity of polymer matrix; rather, it arises from complex microstructural factors incorporating void evolution, interfacial interactions, and nanofiller aggregation. However, the existing models are insufficient to reveal such time-dependent electromechanical mechanisms of VPNs. This work presents a multiphysically-based micromechanical model to elucidate the time-evolutionary piezoresistive behavior of VPNs. Firstly, the geometric configuration of multiwalled carbon nanotubes (MWCNTs) with interfacial interactions and percolation conductivity networks are constructed at the microscale; and then porous microstructures and non-uniform nanofiller aggregation are homogenized at the mesoscale; consequently, the macroscale effective mechanical and electrical properties of VPNs are derived based on Mori-Tanaka and Maxwell far-field methods, respectively. Besides, the post deformation temporal creep and relaxation of electrical resistance are characterized under both step and cyclic loading conditions in experiment, and further validated the proposed model using theoretical calculation and numerical simulation. Furthermore, this model explicitly describes the impacts of the evolution of void aspect ratio and porosity on electromechanical performances of VPNs, and the predicted results of the evolution trends of voids are consistent with the observed time-dependent piezoresistive behaviors. Based on the proposed model, piezoresistive accuracy and stability of VPNs-based flexible sensors can be enhanced by tailoring the viscoelasticity of polymer matrix via the four Burgers parameters. The outcome of this work can provide theoretical guidance for the optimal design of high-performance sensing composites.</p>
