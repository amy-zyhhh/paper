# Strengthening the transition zone of an additively manufactured hybrid TPMS structure for bone scaffold applications

## 摘要

<p>Hybrid TPMS structures can effectively mimic the heterogeneous morphology of human bone and are suitable candidates for scaffold applications. However, their use is limited by the weak transition zone and multidirectional loading in vivo. In this study, skeletal TPMS structures have been fabricated with stainless steel (SS316L) using laser powder bed fusion (LPBF). Compressive behaviors of two uniform TPMS; gyroid and diamond, and their hybrid configuration were examined. More than 97% reduction in effective stiffness was achieved by introducing 60% architected porosity in the bulk material. The structural dependence of local deformation was analyzed through digital image correlation (DIC). The transition zone of the hybrid TPMS structure was strengthened by tuning a mathematical sigmoid function that controls the transition steepness and shape of the transition. The loading direction had a significant effect on the compliance of the structure. More than 50% variation in effective stiffness was observed when the loading orientation was changed in the orthogonal direction. For a vertically hybridized TPMS, higher transition steepness resulted in more uniform local deformation behavior. The biocompatibility and osteoconductivity of the scaffolds were evaluated in vitro. All the scaffold groups have shown good cell proliferation and osteogenic potential on their surfaces. A novel methodology was proposed to tailor the transition parameters such that a hybrid TPMS scaffold behaves like a uniform one under physiological loading. The onset of stress concentration at the hybrid zone can be delayed by tailoring the transition parameters accordingly. The design strategy can be utilized to reduce stress shielding and stimulate uniform bone ingrowth inside a hybrid TPMS scaffold.</p>
