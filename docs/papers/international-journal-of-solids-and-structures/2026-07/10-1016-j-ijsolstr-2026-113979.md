# Computational homogenization of an initially orthotropic compressible structure using kinematic hardening law to model primary and secondary stages of creep

## 摘要

<p>Creep deformation is a critical consideration in designing structures subjected to constant or cyclic loading. Both primary and secondary creep stages are simulated with a unified constitutive law. The compressibility is a consequence of the presence of voids in the structure. A key observation at microscopic scale is that voids do not affect the transition time between primary and secondary creep stages. But their effect is observed both on the primary and secondary creep strain rate. Qualitative analysis of the simulations indicates a direct correlation between void volume and macroscopic strain; larger voids result in greater strain and faster strain rate. Within a thermodynamical framework, an anisotropic compressible kinematic hardening model for initially anisotropic materials was developed. This developed model was applied throughout computational homogenization to a Representative Volume Element (RVE) of a masonry without mortar joints. The homogeneous material parameters are determined using a multistart algorithm in conjunction with the Levenberg–Marquardt optimization method. The homogenized model efficiently captures the orthotropic compressible behavior due to creep of this masonry’s RVE.</p>
