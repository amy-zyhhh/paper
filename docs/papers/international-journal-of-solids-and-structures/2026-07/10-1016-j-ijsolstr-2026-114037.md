# Elastoplastic constitutive model for concrete based on the cohesive-frictional deformation mechanism

## 摘要

<p>This paper presents a novel decoupled cohesive-frictional elastoplastic constitutive model for concrete that captures the coupled evolution and deformation characteristics of cohesive and frictional phases. Concrete is conceptualized as a two-phase material: a cohesive phase described by a hydrostatic-insensitive yield function with an equivalent plastic shear strain-controlled hardening/softening law, and a frictional phase governed by a cap-type yield function with hardening driven by plastic volumetric strain. Three combination schemes, namely series, parallel, and hybrid, are formulated to describe the interaction between the two phases, and the hybrid scheme is adopted to ensure consistency with the intrinsic deformation mechanisms of concrete. The model is parsimonious, involving nine material parameters with clear physical meaning, all obtainable from standard laboratory test. Validation against conventional triaxial tests on concretes of different grades and confining pressures shows that the model accurately captures peak strength, strain softening, and confining-pressure effects under multiaxial stress states. It also captures the progressive transition from cohesive-dominated to frictional-dominated behavior. The model describes both the independent mechanical evolution of the two phases and the resulting macroscopic response, enabling a clearer interpretation of their respective roles. Furthermore, the model can be reduced to purely frictional or purely cohesive model, rendering it potentially applicable to a wide range of geomaterials, including soils and rocks.</p>
