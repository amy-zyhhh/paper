# An effective method for solving three-dimensional frictional contact problems of multilayer materials considering multiscale rough morphological features

## 摘要

<p>A novel mechanical model is developed to characterize the frictional contact behavior of multilayer structures, accounting for multiscale geometric morphological features and internal debonding. First, a geometric multiscale representation model is proposed to decompose and reconstruct the interface geometric morphology, including the macroscopic dimensional deviation, mesoscopic corrugated morphology and microscopic roughness. Then, an equivalent coating method is proposed to represent the stress transfer behavior between the internal debonded layers by using the statistical approach for contact analysis. Next, a three-dimensional contact model is developed to describe the interaction between a multilayer half-space system and an indenter with mixed geometric features, where internal debonding is represented by the equivalent coating. A fast and efficient semi-analytical solution is obtained through Fourier integral transforms and influence coefficient method. Finally, the present contact model is validated by comparing simulation results with existing theoretical results and finite element simulation with two simplified cases. Further results demonstrate the significant influence of multiscale roughness on the contact behavior in multilayer coating systems. This work provides a feasible and effective framework for analyzing the contact response of coating systems with complex multiscale surface morphologies.</p>
