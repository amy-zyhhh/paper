## 摘要

<p>Elastic wave propagating in fluid or solid embedded in infinite poroelastic media can provide critical information about geometry and physical properties, making it an important research topic in non-destructive testing, structural health monitoring, and geophysical exploration. While analytical methods can provide theoretical analysis, they are limited to simple geometries. Equivalent methods can handle complex structures but neglect the interaction between the pore fluid and solid skeleton, thereby losing essential physical information of poroelastic media. To overcome these challenges, this paper proposes a semi-analytical finite element (SAFE) method combined with perfectly matched layer (PML) to compute guided wave propagation characteristics in fluid or solid embedded in infinite poroelastic media. The SAFE-PML method is applied to two representative cases: a fluid-filled borehole and a steel pipe embedded in infinite poroelastic formation. Compared with analytical methods and equivalent methods, the proposed method can analyze the guided wave dispersion characteristics in fluid or solid with arbitrary cross section embedded in infinite poroelastic media, without loss of critical physical information</p>
