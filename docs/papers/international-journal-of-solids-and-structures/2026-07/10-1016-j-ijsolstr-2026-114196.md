# From single crystals to polycrystals via the finite strain multi-phase HFGMC micromechanics

## 摘要

<p>The finite strain generalized method of cells (HFGMC) micromechanical analysis is presently extended and applied for the prediction of the response of polycrystals from the knowledge of the behavior of their randomly oriented viscoplastic single crystal aggregates. To this end, it is necessary to employ two scale procedures which are referred to as local (crystallographic) and global (macroscopic) analyses. In the local analysis, the first tangent tensor of everyone of the single crystal aggregate is established. In the global one, the applied far-field and type of loading are specified, and the multi-phase HFGMC micromechanical analysis is employed in which the established first tangent tensors form the composite’s constituents. This micromechanical analysis provides the instantaneous field distribution among the polycrystal grains, as well as effective tangent tensor of the polycrystal from which its macroscopic behavior can be easily determined. Applications are given for polycrystals which are modeled as doubly and triply periodic composites. The macroscopic response of the polycrystal and the field distribution among its single crystals are shown together with the effect its grains number. Initial yielding of the grains, activity of the slip systems, accumulated plastic strain of the grains, comparisons with the predictions based on Taylor uniform strain assumption, and the behavior of fiber-reinforced polycystals are shown and discussed. The evolution with applied loading of the rotation angles in the doubly periodic polycrystals, and the accumulated and net rotations in the triply periodic case are presented.</p>
