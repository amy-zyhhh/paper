# Adhesion modulation induced by surface roughness and electric load in piezoelectric materials

## 摘要

<p>It is well-known that real surfaces are rarely uniform or smooth at the microscale, which often have some degree of roughness. Exploring the influence mechanism of surface roughness on adhesion has always been a research hotspot in the field of adhesive contact mechanics. However, existing works only focus on the rough adhesion behaviors of isotropic elastic materials. In this paper, the axisymmetric frictionless rough adhesive contact problem between a piezoelectric half-space and a rigid spherical indenter with wavy surface and different electric properties is investigated. By virtue of the superposition principle and Griffith energy balance criterion, the classical Johnson-Kendall-Roberts (JKR) and Maugis-Dugdale (M-D) models are extended to study the rough adhesion behaviors of piezoelectric materials for the first time. The analytical solutions of the physical quantities at the contact surface and the relationships among the indentation force, contact radius and indentation depth are obtained. The effects of electric load, surface roughness and piezoelectric effect on adhesion behaviors are revealed. It is found that the presence of surface roughness leads to the oscillation of equilibrium curves and yields irreversible energy dissipation due to unstable jumps, which thus results in a larger pull-off force compared with smooth case. Exerting electric load can enhance the adhesion effect between the spherical indenter and piezoelectric half-space for both of the smooth and rough cases, which indicates that the presence of surface roughness does not alter the effect of electric load on adhesion behavior. In addition, the pull-off force corresponding to rough case can accomplish the transition from Derjaguin-Muller-Toporov (DMT) solution to JKR solution as the transition parameter continuously increases. Furthermore, it is shown that adhesion enhancement induced by surface roughness and the influence of piezoelectric effect on pull-off force are primarily confined to the JKR region. The results derived from this work are helpful to understand microscale rough adhesion behaviors of piezoelectric materials.</p>
