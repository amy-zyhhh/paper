## 摘要

<p>In nuclear, chemical, and transportation industries, dynamic crack propagation and branching of pipelines may be triggered by accidental internal explosions. From the mesoscopic perspective, this is the result of microvoids nucleation and evolution in the region ahead of crack tips. However, macroscopic damage models fail to capture the detailed evolution of microvoids, and the underlying mesoscopic mechanism of dynamic fracture remains unclear. To bridge this knowledge gap, the Gurson-Tvergaard-Needleman (GTN) mesoscopic damage model was incorporated into the Johnson-Cook thermo-viscoplastic constitutive model to reveal the mesoscopic mechanism of dynamic crack propagation and branching. The fluid-structure interaction between internal detonation wave and pipe was achieved using the immersed boundary method (IBM) and softened contact algorithm. Results demonstrated that the developed model effectively described void nucleation, growth and void volume fraction (VVF) evolution. The increase of VVF at the main crack tip was primarily governed by void nucleation (VVFN). Conversely, at the branched crack tip, it was mainly driven by void growth (VVFG). Results further revealed that the simultaneous occurrence of void nucleation and growth at the crack tips led to following straight crack propagation. In contrast, crack branching developed when void growth occurred after the void nucleation had reached 55%-70% of its peak value. In this case, the crack branching angle was consistent with the direction of both the maximum VVFG and strain rate in the region ahead of crack tip. The study provides new insights into the mesoscopic mechanism of dynamic crack propagation of pipe due to internal explosive loading.</p>
