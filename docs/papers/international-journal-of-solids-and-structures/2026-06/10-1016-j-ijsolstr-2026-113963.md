# Digital programming of deformation and energy absorption in TPMS metamaterials via binary image encoding

## 摘要

<p>This study presents a programmable design methodology for triply periodic minimal surface (TPMS) metamaterials that integrates negative Poisson’s ratio (NPR) and positive Poisson’s ratio (PPR) unit cells via binary image encoding. By spatially arranging NPR and PPR architectures within a digital matrix, the mechanical properties—including stiffness, energy absorption, and deformation behavior—can be precisely tailored to target specifications. Experimental and numerical analyses demonstrate that the PPR‑reinforced NPR configuration increases the elastic modulus by 247% (307.7 ± 21.2 MPa) while achieving a specific energy absorption of 12.2 ± 0.8 J/g. The binary encoding framework enables programmable control of Poisson’s ratio (−0.8 to 0.38), bending angle α (6°–25°), and shear angle θ (9°–36°). This work establishes a proof-of-concept programmable design strategy for tailoring the mechanical performance and deformation behaviors of CLP-Gyroid hybrid TPMS systems, and the proposed strategy advances metamaterial development in energy absorption engineering and adaptive deformation systems.</p>
