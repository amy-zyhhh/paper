# Theoretical and numerical analysis of arbitrarily shaped planar cracks in 2D hexagonal piezoelectric quasicrystals

## 摘要

<p>This study provides a comprehensive theoretical and numerical analysis of arbitrarily shaped planar cracks in two-dimensional (2D) hexagonal piezoelectric quasicrystals (PQCs). The theoretical framework is established based on Fabrikant’s analysis method and the hypersingular integral equation method, through which conventional boundary integral equations are transformed into hypersingular forms. This formulation enables an analysis of the asymptotic singularity behavior induced by coupled electrical-phonon-phason fields, and the corresponding singular solutions are derived. Building on this analysis, the extended stress intensity factors are expressed, and their relationships with the energy release rate are established. To support the theoretical results, a numerical scheme based on the extended displacement discontinuity (EDD) boundary element method is developed. The crack is discretized into triangular elements, and the associated fundamental solutions are constructed applying the principle of linear superposition. The validity and effectiveness of the proposed numerical approach are demonstrated through representative numerical examples. The results show that this present method can effectively predict the behavior of arbitrarily shaped planar cracks in 2D hexagonal PQCs, offering a robust framework for fracture analysis in PQCs under multi-field coupling conditions.</p>
