# MPA-optimized conv-NPR honeycomb metamaterial: Multi-resonant coupling for low-freq broadband near-perfect absorption

## 摘要

<p>Low-frequency noise control in lightweight engineering structures remains a significant challenge, particularly constrained by the poor low-frequency absorption performance and excessive thickness of conventional porous materials. To overcome these limitations, this study develops a systematic methodology for designing broadband high-performance acoustic metamaterials based on a multi-resonant coupling mechanism. A hybrid design strategy is proposed by integrating conventional, semi-re-entrant, and fully re-entrant negative Poisson’s ratio honeycomb units with Helmholtz resonators, thereby constructing a novel Multi-phase composite Honeycomb-cored Acoustic Metamaterial (MHAM). The geometric parameters are optimized via a bio-inspired Marine Predators Algorithm (MPA). Theoretical modeling founded on acoustic impedance theory and finite element simulations demonstrates that the optimized structure achieves continuous high-efficiency sound absorption (α ≥ 0.9) over the 433 Hz to 610 Hz range, with simultaneous coverage of a 50% half-power bandwidth and a 36% high-efficiency bandwidth within the 200–800 Hz band. The broadband enhancement mechanism is attributed to the parallel coupling of multiple resonant units, which merges discrete absorption peaks into a continuous near-perfect absorption band. Experimental validation using an impedance tube shows excellent agreement with numerical predictions. Furthermore, a comprehensive parametric analysis clarifies how key geometric variables—cell height, panel thickness, core thickness, and pore diameters—independently control absorption characteristics, providing clear guidance for inverse design. This work establishes a versatile methodology for developing lightweight, stiff, and acoustically tunable honeycomb-cored metamaterials, offering a promising approach for integrated structural–acoustic design in noise-sensitive thin-walled applications.</p>
