## 摘要

<p>The geometry of a construction element can have a significant impact on the mechanical performance of the designed assembly. This direct relation between the microstructure and mechanical properties is strongly manifested in the field of material engineering, structural design and technology of metamaterials. This work presents a unique, space filling octahedron, along with a number of interesting structures which can be assembled using this specific polyhedron. In particular, a 24-faces, space filling solid which resembles an adamantane cage and which enables the construction of diamond-like superstructures. The symmetry of the object ensures uniform spatial force redistribution in a stack and thus makes it a perfect candidate to design constructions exposed to demanding loads or damage. 3D printed models are experimentally examined. Furthermore, a new truss architecture, based on the space filling octahedron, is introduced and evaluated, both experimentally and computationally, in terms of its strength and stability. Moreover, the Young’s modulus of the materials and displacement of the structures are experimentally determined on a test stand.</p>
