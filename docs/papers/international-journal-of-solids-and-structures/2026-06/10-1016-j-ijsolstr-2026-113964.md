# A hyperelastic constitutive model for compressible particle-reinforced neo-Hookean elastomers with interphases at finite deformation

## 摘要

<p>This paper presents systematic model framework based on micromechanics to characterize the macroscopic response of compressible particle-reinforced composites with multilayered interphases under finite deformations. For the sake of simplicity, all constituent phases of the composites are modeled as neo-Hookean hyperelastic materials with different material properties in this framework. Based on the analysis of numerical homogenization results, we introduce a constitutive closure hypothesis where the macroscopic constitutive response of the composites is postulated to retain the functional form of their individual constituent phases. On this basis, the classical composite-sphere model and three-phase model are respectively generalized to predict the effective material parameters of the proposed model, namely the effective shear modulus μ ¯ and the effective bulk modulus k ¯ . To assess the performance of the developed constitutive model, a comprehensive series of numerical simulations are conducted. Specifically, the effects of particle volume fraction, interphase thickness, interphase-to-matrix stiffness ratio, number of interphase layers and various loading conditions on the macroscopic response of the composites are first investigated under incompressible conditions, followed by a detailed discussion on the compressible hyperelastic behaviors of the composites. The predictions of the macroscopic constitutive model are in excellent agreement with the numerical homogenization results across all considered cases. Finally, validation against experimental data reported in the literature further verifies the applicability and effectiveness of the proposed model.</p>
