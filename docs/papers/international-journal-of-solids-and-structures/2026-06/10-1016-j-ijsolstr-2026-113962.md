# An anisotropic damage model for the mechanical and chemo-mechanical coupling behaviors of double-network hydrogels

## 摘要

<p>Double-network (DN) hydrogels exhibit outstanding mechanical properties, yet they display complex behaviors such as necking and stress softening under large deformations. To date, constitutive models have rarely been applied to simultaneously describe both the mechanical responses and the chemo–mechanical coupling behaviors, especially when additional factors, such as deformation modes, are considered. In this work, an anisotropic damage model is developed. For a highly heterogeneous filler network, both the chain length distribution and the orientation distribution of polymer chains are considered. In addition, a non-affine micro–macro mapping, combining the classical eight-chain model with the affine full-network model, is adopted, which is the key to accurately characterizing the degree of anisotropy. The progressive damage of material is captured through the evolution of the probability density–chain length distribution. Moreover, the model incorporates the post-damage swelling behavior of DN gels by analyzing the stress equilibrium and chemical potential equilibrium. The developed model captures various mechanical/chemo-mechanical responses well, including stress softening under various deformation modes and stretch ratio levels, the reloading stress along different directions, and the anisotropic swelling behavior. For several essential indicators, i.e., the directional modulus reduction factor, the directional energy dissipation factor, and the swelling anisotropy factor, the model predictions are in good agreement with the experimental results. The ability of the developed model to predict both mechanical and chemo-mechanical coupling behaviors provides critical insights for the rational design and practical application of DN gels.</p>
