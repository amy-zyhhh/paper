# Impact dynamics of three-dimensional horseshoe microstructure lattice under indentation deformation

## 摘要

<p>Owing to its exceptional flexibility, designability, and capacity to mitigate peak impact forces, horseshoe microstructure lattice has shown great potential for personal protection. However, considering the geometric diversity of impactors in practical applications, lattice structures may exhibit distinct indentation deformation under impact, which could significantly affect their energy absorption capacity. To address this, in the present paper, the dynamic response of three-dimensional horseshoe microstructure lattice (THML) under indentation deformation was investigated, with the indentation geometry being primarily governed by the configuration of projectile. Based on the deformation modes of single beams, a theoretical model was developed to predict the energy absorption capacities of the THML under indentation deformation. It is revealed that under impact loading, the beams undergo severe fracture, with fracture strain exhibits a positive correlation with arc angle, indicating enhanced flexibility at larger curvatures. Furthermore, THML exhibits two distinct deformation stages, the first stage mainly involves indentation deformation, followed by uniform compression stage. For THML with small arc angles, the external energy was primarily absorbed through indentation deformation. Furthermore, the energy absorption mechanisms of THML is significantly influenced by indentation geometry. Compared to pyramidal indentations, the THML under spherical indentations exhibits superior energy absorption capacity via beam compression. Moreover, as the maximum indentation depth increases, energy absorbed by beam tension in THML is progressively enhanced, whereas the reaction force exhibits a corresponding reduction.</p>
