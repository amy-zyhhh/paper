# Buckling behavior of 3D-printed Beetle Elytron-inspired cellular structures with regular and auxetic honeycomb cores: Experimental and numerical study

## 摘要

<p>This study investigates the in-plane buckling behavior of bio-inspired cellular structures, focusing on regular (HBEP) and inverted (IHBEP) Honeycomb Beetle Elytron Plate designs and benchmarking them against their conventional honeycomb (HP) and inverted honeycomb (IHP) counterparts. The combined effects of Beetle Elytron-inspired trabeculae and auxetic cell walls are examined for the first time using a coupled experimental–numerical approach based on 3D-printed specimens and nonlinear Riks simulations. Experiments show that HBEP and IHBEP cores significantly increase the critical buckling loads compared to conventional honeycombs. In sandwich panels, the improvement is less pronounced due to the dominant structural role of the facesheets. Notably, BEP-inspired designs delay local facesheet instabilities and mitigate facesheet–core interface fracture in the post-buckling regime. A factorial numerical design of experiments highlights the interaction between facesheet and cell-wall slenderness, showing that the highest performance gains of IHBEP over IHP are achieved with thin facesheets and thick cell walls. In addition, IHBEP promotes a transition toward global buckling, reducing failure risks such as facesheet–core interface damage. Finally, empirical regression models are proposed to efficiently predict the critical buckling loads, providing a practical design tool across a wide range of configurations.</p>
