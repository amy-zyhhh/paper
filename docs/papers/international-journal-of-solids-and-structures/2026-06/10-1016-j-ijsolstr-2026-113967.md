## 摘要

<p>Diamond and silicon carbide (SiC) exhibit excellent mechanical, electrical and thermal properties, which is ideal for thermal management in advanced electronics. This study employs large-scale molecular dynamics simulations to investigate the shear properties and failure mechanisms at the nano-diamond (ND)/SiC interface. The interface characterized by the formation of C-C sp3 bonds, exhibits a brittle failure feature. It is found that higher strain rates increase shear strength marginally, while higher temperatures result in a reduced shear strength. Interestingly, the residual stress is found to oscillate after fracture, which is attributed to the free vibration of the SiC region. Additionally, empirical potentials are found to exert profound effects on the interfacial bonding and failure patterns. These insights provide critical guidance for designing high-performance diamond/SiC composites for advanced electronics.</p>
