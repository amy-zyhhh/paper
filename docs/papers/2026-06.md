## Data-driven homogenization of architected metamaterials with nonlinear microstructure

<p class="meta"><strong>Authors:</strong> Arjun Patel, Sofia Martinez, Ellen Kuhl</p>
<p class="meta"><strong>Date:</strong> 2026-06-24</p>
<p class="meta"><strong>Journal:</strong> Journal of the Mechanics and Physics of Solids - Volume 197 - Article 105433</p>
<p class="meta"><strong>DOI:</strong> 10.1016/j.jmps.2026.105433</p>
<p class="meta"><strong>Keywords:</strong> metamaterials, homogenization, data-driven mechanics, surrogate modeling</p>
<p class="meta"><strong>Topics:</strong> metamaterials, homogenization</p>

A data-driven framework is developed for predicting the effective response of architected metamaterials with nonlinear microstructural behavior. The method combines numerical homogenization with reduced-order surrogate modeling.

## Crystal plasticity analysis of size effects in gradient nanostructured metals

<p class="meta"><strong>Authors:</strong> Yuki Tanaka, Ming Li, Robert McMeeking</p>
<p class="meta"><strong>Date:</strong> 2026-06-12</p>
<p class="meta"><strong>Journal:</strong> Journal of the Mechanics and Physics of Solids - Volume 197 - Article 105434</p>
<p class="meta"><strong>DOI:</strong> 10.1016/j.jmps.2026.105434</p>
<p class="meta"><strong>Keywords:</strong> crystal plasticity, size effects, nanostructured metals, dislocations</p>
<p class="meta"><strong>Topics:</strong> plasticity, dislocations</p>

The influence of microstructural length scales on the plastic deformation of gradient nanostructured metals is investigated using a crystal plasticity framework with geometrically necessary dislocations.
